DNF

Did Not Finish

Did Not Finish, abbreviated DNF, indicates that a driver did not finish the race. This DNF may be caused by an accident or a technical problem that prevents the driver from finishing the race or forces them to abandon it.

DSQ

Disqualified

Disqualified, abbreviated DSQ, indicates that a driver is excluded from the race classification. This disqualification can occur before, during, or after the race has finished.

Course Sprint

In Formula 1, we can find sprint races in addition to classic races. These sprint races are more intense, so we can see more battles and overtaking. These sprint races are about 100 km long and there are no mandatory pit stops. During the 2026 season, there will be six sprint races.

DRS

Drag Reduction System

Drag Reduction System, abbreviated DRS, is a mechanism that allows the rear wing to be temporarily modified during the race to increase speed. It can only be used in specific areas and only when the driver is less than 1 second behind another driver. DRS was introduced in Formula 1 in 2011 and will be removed in 2026.

ERS

Energy Recovery System

Energy Recovery System, abbreviated ERS. This system allows the heat produced when the single-seater brakes to be recovered and converted into energy. This energy is stored in a battery and can be reused to go faster and thus help with overtaking.
